# Notes: UTMA Accounts and Kiddie Tax

Date: 2026-03-15
Tags: personal-finance

## What is a UTMA Account?

A UTMA (Uniform Transfers to Minors Act) account is a custodial account
that lets an adult transfer assets to a minor, with the adult acting as
custodian until the minor reaches the age of majority (18 or 21 depending
on the state). Unlike a 529 plan, funds in a UTMA can be used for
anything that benefits the minor — not just education.

## Kiddie Tax Rules

Unearned income (interest, dividends, capital gains) in a UTMA account
above a certain threshold is taxed at the *parent's* marginal tax rate,
not the child's. The first portion of unearned income is tax-free, the
next portion is taxed at the child's rate, and anything above that is
taxed at the parent's rate. This is the "Kiddie Tax" and exists to prevent
parents from shifting investment income to children to get a lower rate.

## 529 vs UTMA Tradeoff

- **529**: tax-free growth *if* used for qualified education expenses.
  Counted as a parental asset for FAFSA purposes (less impact on aid
  eligibility than a UTMA, which counts as the student's own asset).
- **UTMA**: more flexible (any use for the minor's benefit), but counted
  more heavily against financial aid eligibility, and subject to Kiddie
  Tax on growth.

## Conclusion from Planning Discussion

Given an existing 529 is already funded for education, redirecting
additional UTMA contributions toward a different vehicle (e.g., paying
down a loan, or a custodial brokerage outside UTMA) may make more sense
than growing the UTMA further, given the FAFSA and Kiddie Tax
considerations — especially since the UTMA's flexibility (non-education
use) is less valuable if education is already covered by the 529.
